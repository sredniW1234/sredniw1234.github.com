Quick Overview of the Circulatory System:

* This is somewhat of a TLDR * The circulatory system is the system in which blood is pumped around the body delivering vital resources to cells.

1.  What is it?

The circulatory system is an essential part of the body which regulates all the blood and where that blood goes. Without it, you would cease to live because there would be no blood pumping throughout your body. Its main purpose is to provide essential resources to the cells in your body.

2.  What are the parts?

There are three main parts of the Circulatory system:

2.1  The Heart:

The heart is a very complex organ of the human body. It has the purpose of pumping the blood to and from the body. The heart has multiple valves in order to let blood pass through in one direction but not another.

2.2  The Blood:

The blood is the actual liquid that is transported through the body. It contains two different type of cells, red and white blood cells. Red blood cells are the cells that carry the resources such as oxygen and nutrients. White blood cells help fight off bacteria and infections that are unwanted or harmful.

2.3  Arteries:

Arteries are the main blood vessel that deliver oxygen rich blood to your cells. That's why it is a critical emergency when you puncture, block, or clog an artery because it is the vessel that transports the oxygen to your cells.

2.4  Veins:

Veins are the other type of blood vessel. Their main function is to return the blood from your organs back to the heart. The blood that they carry aren't oxygenated but instead deoxygenated. This in simple terms means that it lacks oxygen and instead carries carbon dioxide.

2.5  Capillaries:

Last but not least, we have the Capillaries. Capillaries are the smallest and the most vast compared to the other blood vessels. They are what actually distribute the oxygen to the cells.

3.  Where does it start?

The circulatory system could be considered tp start at the heart. The heart is where the blood gets pumped from. So because it's the origin point of movement, one could consider it the start.

4.  How does it work?

We start at the heart, blood gets pumped through the heart and to the lungs and transfers gasses. From here, the now oxygen rich blood then goes back to the heart and through the various valves. The blood then gets distributed throughout the body, through the various arteries, capillaries, and veins. The blood then flows back to the heart to restart the cycle.
