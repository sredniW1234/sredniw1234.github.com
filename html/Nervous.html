Quick Overview of the Nervous System:

* This is somewhat of a TLDR * The nervous system is a system which allows the body to be controlled and have sensations.

1.  What is it?

The nervous system is responsible for any sensation, movement, and instinct you have inside your body. The nervous system also allows every body part to move, including sight, smell, breath, think, and more.

2.  What are the parts?

The main components of the nervous system are the brain, spinal cord, motor neurons, sensory neurons, and the nerves.

2.1  The Brain:

The brain is what controls the entire body, from movement, to thinking, to breathing. The brain is extremely complex, so much so that we don't know a lot about how the brain works. The brain controls, along with the spinal cord, makes up the central nervous system.

2.2  The Spinal Cord:

The spinal cord is a cord that connects your brain all the way down to your lower back. It carries important neural signals to and from the brain. That's why it's critical whenever you injure your spine as it can cause paralysis due to the cut of neural signals.

2.3  The Motor Neurons:

Motor neurons are neurons that control your muscles, organs, and glands in your body. These carry specific signals from the brain and spinal cord to actuate your muscles. These movements can either be voluntary or involuntary.

2.4  The Sensory Neurons:

Sensory neurons detect changes in the environment, either chemically or physically. When this happens, the neurons send a signal to the spinal cord to relay to the brain for processing.

3.  Where does it start?

The nervous system starts from the brain transmits information to the spinal cord. The spinal cord uses the motor neurons to send the information to the muscles.

4.  How does it work?

The brain makes the responses, movement, emotions, speaking, and everything else that makes a human and sends that information to the spinal cord. The spinal cord uses the motor neurons to transportation the information from the brain to the body and back. The spinal cord uses the motor neurons to send the information to the muscles to make the muscles move and act in a way we move. The sensory neuron carries information about changes in the environment.

5.  Where does it end?

The ending of the nervous system, ends when the changes in the environment signals are sent back to the brain. Resulting to any movements done.
