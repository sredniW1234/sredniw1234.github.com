Quick Overview of the Excretory System:

* This is somewhat of a TLDR * The excretory system is a system where bodily waste products are removed.

1.  What is it?

The excretory system is the body system that handles the disposal of bodily wastes. These wastes range from excess water and urine, to nitrogenous wastes in the blood and carbon dioxide.

2.  What are the parts?

The main parts of the urinary excretory system include: two kidneys, two ureters, a bladder, and a urethra.

The Kidneys:

The kidneys is an organ in the body that filters wastes from the blood. As the blood passes through the kidneys, they remove a waste known as urea from the blood. This is then formed into urine.

The Ureters:

The ureters are narrow tubes that carry the urine created from the kidneys to the bladder. They do this via a process called peristalsis which is the process of contracting and relaxing muscles to move the contents of, in this case the ureters, forward.

The Bladder:

The bladder is a sac that functions to hold urine from the kidneys that was passed by the ureters. The bladder holds the urine so that it can be excreted by the urethra at a later time.

The Urethra:

The urethra is a tube that functions for releasing urine from the bladder.

3.  Aren't there other organs that deal with waste management?

Yes, there are other organs that remove other types of wastes from the body such as the anus or sweat glands however, they belong to other systems. This means that although they are part of the excretory system, they won't be covered here.

4.  Where does it start?

The excretory starts in the kidneys. This is where the wastes are first removed and processed from the blood to be converted into urine.

5.  How does it work?

The kidneys remove the nitrogenous waste via small filters called nephrons. According to the University of Rochester Medical Center, there are approximately one million of these nephrons in each kidney, equating to 2 million total. These quickly sort out the urea from the blood and makes urine. The ureters the take the urine to the bladder to be held. Finally, when the time comes, the urethra releases the urine, removing the waste from the body.
