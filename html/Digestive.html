Quick Overview of the Digestive System:

* This is somewhat of a TLDR * The digestive system is a system where nutrients are absorbed into the body from the foods you eat.

1.  What is it?

The digestive system is where the food or liquid we consume enters the body and is transformed into energy. Without the digestive system, we wouldn't have the energy and nutrients to live our lives.

2.  What are the parts?

Major components of the Digestive Systems are the mouth, esophagus, stomach, small intestine, large intestine, and the anus.

The Esophagus:

The esophagus is a part of a canal that transports food. Its main purpose is to connect the mouth and the stomach. It also can be referred to as the gullet. It is usually lined with mucous in order to help lubricate the bolus so that it can be passed down easier.

The Stomach:

The stomach is the second place where mechanical digestion happens. The stomach churns and mixes pepsin, an enzyme produced by the stomach, to continue to digest the food. In the stomach, the main function of pepsin is to break down proteins into smaller components such as peptides and amino acids.

The Small Intestine:

Is a tube that is about twenty feet in length in which it connects the stomach and large intestine. In the small intestine, chemical digestion occurs. This means that the intestine absorbs the nutrients and minerals while also getting rid of anything that is not needed.

The Large Intestine:

The large intestine's main function is to absorb any water that might be left in the food. It also absorbs vitamins and forming feces. It is the tube between the small intestine and the anus. Once the food gets to the end of the large intestine, the large intestine pushes the feces to the anus.

The Anus:

The anus is the final destination of the food. Once it passes the large intestine, the anus does controllable bowel movements to push out the what now is feces.

3.  Where does it start?

The digestive starts within the mouth when we consume foods and drinks and begin a mechanical digestion, when the food is smaller we begin to swallow the remains into our body. This starts the transformation with the remaining food turning it into amino acids, carbohydrates, proteins, or lipids for the body.

4.  How does it work?

Foods and liquids are digested into our body and turned into simpler forms, forms such as sugars, or proteins in our body. And the rest becomes a waste product disposed within the anus of the body after going through the large and small intestine.

5.  Where does it end?

The remaining food transforms into a feces within the large intestine, which follows a path into the small intestine and then released from the anus.
