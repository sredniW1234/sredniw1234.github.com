Quick Overview of the Digestive System:

* This is somewhat of a TLDR * The Digestive System is a system where nutrients are absorbed into the body from the foods you eat.

1.  What is it?

The digestive system is where the food or liquid we consume enters the body and is transformed into energy. Without the digestive system, we wouldn't have the energy and nutrients to live our lives.

2.  What are the parts?

Major components of the Digestive Systems are the mouth, esophagus, stomach, small intestine, large intestine, and the anus.

The esophagus:

The ureters are narrow tubes that carry the urine created from the kidneys to the bladder. They do this via a process called peristalsis which is the process of contracting and relaxing muscles to move the contents of, in this case the ureters, forward.

The bladder:

The bladder is a sac that functions to hold urine from the kidneys that was passed by the ureters. The bladder holds the urine so that it can be excreted by the urethra at a later time.

The urethra:

The urethra is a tube that functions for releasing urine from the bladder.

3.  Where does it start?

The digestive starts within the mouth when we consume foods and drinks and begin a mechanical digestion, when the food is smaller we begin to swallow the remains into our body. This starts the transformation with the remaining food turning it into amino acids, carbohydrates, proteins, or lipids for the body.

4.  How does it work?

Foods and liquids are digested into our body and turned into simpler forms, forms such as sugars, or proteins in our body. And the rest becomes a waste product disposed within the anus of the body after going through the large and small intestine.

5.  Where does it end?

The remaining food transforms into a feces within the large intestine, which follows a path into the small intestine and then released from the anus.
