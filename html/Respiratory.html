Quick Overview of the Respiratory System:

* This is somewhat of a TLDR * The Respiratory System is a system in which we inhale oxygen and exhale carbon dioxide in the process called respiration.

1.  What is it?

The respiratory system is a body system that involves breathing. To put it more precisely, breathing is the process of inhaling oxygen and exhaling a waste product called carbon dioxide.

2.  What are the parts?

The main parts of the respiratory system is the mouth/nose, throat, larynx, trachea, bronchi, and the lungs.

2.1  The mouth and nose:

The mouth and nose is where air is taken in from and where carbon dioxide is expelled. Although we can breathe in and out from both, they serve different purposes. The mouth is mainly for eating and speaking whereas the nose is for breathing, filtering debris, and smelling.

2.1  The throat:

The throat is a hollow tube located in the neck. It serves the main function of transporting liquid, foods, and oxygen.

2.1  The larynx:

The larynx, more commonly know as the voice box, helps produce sounds. These sounds can change in pitch and loudness. Using said sounds, people can form different formations of their mouth and tongue to form words and speak.

2.1  The trachea:

The trachea, commonly know to many as the windpipe, is another hollow tube, similar to that of the throat. It carries the inhaled oxygen from the throat into the lungs and the carbon dioxide from the lungs to the throat to be exhaled.

2.1  The lungs:

There are actually two lungs within you, although they do preform the same function. The lungs is the location in which gas exchange between oxygen and carbon dioxide happens. But before then, the oxygen must go through tiny tubes called bronchi and bronchioles.

2.1  The bronchi, bronchioles, and alveoli:

The bronchi are little tubes that carry oxygen into tinier tubes called bronchioles. These bronchioles then lead to minuscule, grape-like sacs called alveoli. Alveoli hold the oxygen in order for said oxygen to swap with the carbon dioxide that is to be exhaled.
