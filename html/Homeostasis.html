Homeostasis

What is it? What does it do? How does it work? And why do we need it?
↓

1. What exactly is homeostasis?

Homeostasis is the BALANCE of the body's state and making sure it stays in balance. It's exact definition goes as follows: "self-regulating process by which biological systems maintain stability while adjusting to changing external conditions"

( ↳ Homeostasis: The Underappreciated and Far Too Often Ignored Central Organizing Principle of Physiology, NIH )

2. What are some examples of?

As mentioned on the home page, it keeps the balance in the body. This means that if there is a physical or chemical imbalance, homeostasis will try to bring it back to the default values. Some examples of this are:

Temperature

Let's say for example that you're sweating. Well, that's homeostasis in action! The reason why you sweat is due to the fact you're too hot so your body tries to cool you down by sweating. Similarly, when you shiver, it's your body trying to warm you up because it's too cold.

Blood glucose levels

If you have too much glucose in your bloodstream, hormones sent by the pancreas called insulin cause it to go down. Another hormone called glucagon raises it when it's too low. Diseases such as diabetes interrupt homeostasis directly by making the pancreas no produce enough insulin causing blood sugar levels to not be balanced.

Waste

If you have ever drunken water, which you most likely have, you would know that eventually, you would need to use the bathroom. This is you're body keeping homeostasis by disposing of the excess waste water that is no longer needed.

3. How does homeostasis actually work?

It starts with stimuli which lets the body know that something is unbalanced. Weather that be too little sugar or too much waste, different receptors detect those changes and send the signals to what's called the control center which is located in the brain. It then chooses the appropriate response to send to what's known as the effector.

4. Why is homeostasis required?

Let's say one day homeostasis were to just disappear, how would you know when something is wrong in your body? The short answer is that you wouldn't until it's too late. Homeostasis, in a way, protects us and manages lots quietly.
